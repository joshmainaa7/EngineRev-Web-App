History of Mercedes

Founder:Karl Benz

Early Years:
Foundation of Mercedes-Benz: The origins of Mercedes-Benz can be traced back to the late 19th century. In 1886, Karl Benz invented the first gasoline-powered automobile, the Benz Patent-Motorwagen. Meanwhile, Gottlieb Daimler and Wilhelm Maybach were developing their own engines and vehicles. The Daimler-Motoren-Gesellschaft (DMG) was founded in 1890 by Daimler and Maybach.

Early Successes: In the early 1900s, both Benz and DMG experienced success in automotive innovation and racing. Benz & Cie. merged with DMG in 1926 to form Daimler-Benz AG, leading to the creation of the Mercedes-Benz brand. The brand name "Mercedes" was derived from the name of a Daimler automobile model and "Benz" from Karl Benz's surname.
